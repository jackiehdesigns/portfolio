Shadow Play: an Inter-generational Playspace

Prosperous Gardens, Yau Ma Tei, Hong Kong

07-10 / 2019

Competition: The Intergenerational Play Space Design Competition organised by the Jockey Club Design Institute for Social Innovation (JCDISI) with the Hong Kong Housing Society (HKHS)

In collaboration with Christina Tsui and Jessie Wan

The competition brief required a master plan for a playscape/ spatial design that creates an inviting environment for elderly users and promotes intergenerational play and interaction. We also needed to select and/or design creative, safe, and easy-to-maintain play equipment as well as fitness equipment to encourage physical excerise of elderly users. The site would be the public space of the housing estate Prosperous Gardens in Yau Ma Tei.

When visiting Prosperous Gardens, we noticed that the majority of elderly users preferred to sit in the spaces under podium as they were well-shaded. This meant that there was limited intergenerational interaction as the children preferred to stay in the playground under the sunshine. Therefore, we wanted to tackle this issue of lack of shaded space and suitable facilities to promote intergenerational interaction.

Our concept consisted of 'shadow play' structures which are circular canopies sitting on top or extending from poles. They are inspired by German playground design and manufacturer Richter Spielger√§te. Shadow play makes use of projecting properties of translucent materials under sunlight to make shadows of various shapes and colors, and these canopies suspended on poles can be rotated and moved vertically by users to overlap and enlarge shadows, hence the 'shadow play'. This colorful and outstanding element stimulates children's attention and creates a playful atmosphere. These canopies also offer a more practical aspect by moving the shade to appropriate areas like over seating for comfort.
