Circulation route forms the basis of building a daily routine of sustainable living

eCo-living Housing

Collective Living for Transitional Housing

Po Lam, Tseung Kwan O, Hong Kong

09-11/2020

Bachelor's Academic Design Studio U5

In collaboration with Erica Kwok, Grace Lee, and Chris Ip

Transitional social housing was recently introduced by the government to provide accommodation for low-income communities as short-term relief. Research and interviews allowed us to understand more about the daily routine of users and with an empathic design approach, reimagine the possibilities with a sustainable consequence in mind to invigorate residential design in Hong Kong. The concept behind our transient community of our collective housing design is centered around building eco-living habits for a better life.

Our site is situated in the Tseung Kwan O district, which is normally perceived as just a residential cluster, and sometimes even has negative associations due to its proximity to landfills. Therefore, our project aims to promote sustainable living and encourage eco-friendly values as a way to empower and connect the community.

The concept of sustainable living is embedded into the inhabitant’s daily routine. The housing clusters are surrounded by ramps that serve as routes for their daily routines. Sustainable collective living programs (i.e. shared kitchens and laundry) are distributed throughout this route. When inhabitants walk along the ramp, they can observe neighbours’ ways of living and through this peer learning, build a more sustainable lifestlye.
